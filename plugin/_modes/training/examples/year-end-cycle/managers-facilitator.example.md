# Facilitator Guide: Managers Training — Year-End 2026

> **Engagement:** ACME Corp
> **Audience:** People managers (mixed seniority — first-cycle through veterans)
> **Delivery target:** Week 0 / Cascade / target date 2026-12-10
> **Total target duration:** 60 minutes
> **Posture:** broadcast-with-checkpoints
> **Companion deck:** `managers.pptx`
> **Companion blocks:** `managers-interactive-blocks.md`

---

## Pre-session setup (5 min before audience arrives)

- Verify projector / Zoom screen-share works
- Open the deck at slide 1
- Open `managers-interactive-blocks.md` in second window (slide 5 quiz, slide 11 retrieval, slide 12 retrieval)
- Have paper backup of cascade prompts (slides 7, 11) — system failure recovery
- Confirm audience size; if ≥40 managers, plan to skip slide 12 retrieval (too large for live retrieval; convert to async)

---

## Slide 1: Cover

**Time:** 0:00–0:01

**Facilitator says:**

> "Welcome. Today's an hour to get you ready to run the cascade kit with your team next week. By the end, you'll know the cycle structure, you'll be able to answer the team questions you'll get, and you'll know what to defer to HRBP."

**Audience does:** Settles in.

**Purpose:** Set the goal — manager prep, not abstract training.

---

## Slide 2: What you walk out with

**Time:** 0:01–0:03

**Facilitator says:**

> "Four things to walk out with: cycle structure, mechanics, cascade kit confidence, escalation routing. If by the end you can't do all four, raise your hand and we'll fix it."

**Audience does:** Listens. Commits to the four-item walkout.

**Purpose:** Concrete goals (not "learn about comp"). Sets expectation for facilitator + audience accountability.

---

## Slide 3: The two-piece raise

**Time:** 0:03–0:08

**Facilitator says:**

> "60/40 split. Most of your team's raises this cycle are primarily market-driven — the market widened, we're defending pay competitiveness. Merit is still in there at 40%, but the dominant story is market. This is a shift from last cycle's 50/50 — that's important context when your team asks why."

**Audience does:** Listens. Notes the 60/40 number. Some will ask follow-up questions about why the change.

**Purpose:** Establish the dominant headline. The shift-from-last-cycle context is the most-likely team question.

**Anticipated questions:**
- "What's compa-ratio? Some of my team is on step pay, not merit." → "Right — for step roles, the market component is the scale lift. The 60/40 framing is for merit-band employees. We'll cover edge cases on slide 6."

**Anti-FAQ:**
- "Why did comp committee pick 60/40?" → "Methodology questions go to comp manager via HRBP queue. Don't speculate."

---

## Slide 4: Why the shift

**Time:** 0:08–0:13

**Facilitator says:**

> "Rejected 70/30 because that would have flattened performance differentiation too much. Rejected 50/50 because the market signals indicated the gap was widening faster than our internal performance variance. 60/40 is the chosen middle. Your team will ask 'why did the split change' — this is your answer."

**Audience does:** Notes the answer. Some will internalize the framing; some will struggle with explaining 'compa-ratio' or 'performance variance' to non-technical team members.

**Purpose:** Equip managers with the rationale. The 'why' question is THE question.

**Anticipated questions:**
- "How do I explain 'performance variance' to my team?" → "Use 'how spread out our performance ratings are' instead of jargon. The deck for your team uses plain language."

---

## Slide 5: Quiz — checkpoint

**Time:** 0:13–0:16

**Facilitator says:**

> "Quick comprehension check. <Reads question.> A, B, C, or D?"

**Audience does:** Picks an answer (silently or via polling tool).

**Facilitator (after):**

> "Right — B, 60/40. If anyone picked C (70/30) or D (40/60), let's quickly walk through what those would mean — the framing matters because if you misremember, your team gets the wrong number."

**Block reference:** `managers-interactive-blocks.md` § Block 1

**Purpose:** Verify the headline number stuck. This is the most common manager misremembering — flag now, fix now.

---

## Slide 6: Top-of-band freeze

**Time:** 0:16–0:22

**Facilitator says:**

> "Top-of-band employees on your team — they're frozen on the market component. They get full merit eligibility. ~47 affected company-wide. The reason matters: their pay is already at band max. They might still feel frustrated; that's the human side."

**Audience does:** Listens. Some managers will recognize specific team members in this category and start mentally preparing the conversation.

**Purpose:** Frame the operational mechanic. Set up the human-conversation handling on slide 8.

**Anticipated questions:**
- "What if my top performer is at top of band — won't they leave?" → "Real risk. Cross-band move is a separate process — flag retention-risk top-of-band to HRBP within 5 business days of cycle close."

**Escalation path:**
- Retention-risk top-of-band: HRBP → Comp Manager (5 business days)

---

## Slide 7: Cascade-prompt slide

**Time:** 0:22–0:25

**Facilitator says:**

> "When you run the cascade kit next week, this is your opening. <Reads the cascade prompt.> Notice it's a question, not an explanation. Your job in the team meeting is to LISTEN first — what your team thinks the structure is. Their framing tells you where to spend the rest of the meeting."

**Audience does:** Notes the cascade prompt. Some will mentally rehearse delivery.

**Purpose:** Train cascade discipline. Manager's instinct will be to explain first; cascade prompts are designed to surface team framing first.

---

## Slide 8: Anticipated questions

**Time:** 0:25–0:32

**Facilitator says:**

> "Three questions you WILL get. <Walks through the Q&A bullets one at a time.> Notice each answer is short — manager doesn't need to over-explain. The 'don't commit to next cycle' answer is critical — that's a future-cycle commitment your team will quote back to you in 12 months."

**Audience does:** Notes each Q&A. Some will write down verbatim; some will internalize the pattern.

**Purpose:** Equip the predictable Q&A. The 'don't commit to next cycle' framing is load-bearing.

---

## Slide 9: Anti-FAQ — what NOT to answer

**Time:** 0:32–0:37

**Facilitator says:**

> "Equally important: what you will NOT answer. Total cycle envelope is confidential. Cross-team comparisons are dead-ends. Methodology debates are comp committee territory. If you find yourself wanting to defend a decision, that's your signal it's HRBP queue territory."

**Audience does:** Notes the boundaries. Some will push back ("but my team really needs to know X") — facilitator handles inline.

**Purpose:** Defensive equip. Managers ad-libbing on confidential context is the highest-risk failure mode.

---

## Slide 10: Effective date + letter timing

**Time:** 0:37–0:42

**Facilitator says:**

> "<Walks through the timeline.> Critical dates: Dec 11 cascade kit lands; Dec 15-19 you run it; Dec 15 letters arrive — note that you don't see your team's letters before they do. That's intentional — privacy. If a team member's letter has an error, route to HRBP queue immediately."

**Audience does:** Notes dates. Some will check their calendars for week-of-Dec-15 conflicts.

**Purpose:** Operational clarity. Timing-question is the second-most-common team question.

---

## Slide 11: Cascade-prompt slide #2

**Time:** 0:42–0:45

**Facilitator says:**

> "Mid-meeting prompt for your team meeting. <Reads.> Plant this 15-20 minutes in. By that point your team has context — they're ready to surface what's actually bugging them. Most teams won't volunteer this without the prompt."

**Audience does:** Notes the prompt placement. Some will internalize the cadence.

**Purpose:** Train cadence discipline. Mid-meeting check-in is the place where the unspoken concern surfaces.

---

## Slide 12: Retrieval prompt — checkpoint

**Time:** 0:45–0:48

**Facilitator says:**

> "Quick check — without looking at the slide, what's the one thing you'll change about how you'd run your team meeting now vs. before this briefing? 30 seconds quiet."

**Audience does:** 30 seconds silent reflection. 1-2 voluntary answers.

**Purpose:** Force commitment. Reflection-converted-to-action is the change moment.

**Block reference:** `managers-interactive-blocks.md` § Block 3

---

## Slide 13: Cascade kit + escalation paths

**Time:** 0:48–0:55

**Facilitator says:**

> "Toolkit + when to escalate. Cascade kit lands Dec 11 — read it before your team meeting. HRBP queue Dec 16+ for everything you can't answer or shouldn't answer. Comp manager: cross-band moves and retention-risk only. Don't escalate to comp manager for general cycle questions; that's queue territory."

**Audience does:** Notes the routing. Some will ask about specific edge cases.

**Purpose:** Operational routing. Last chance to clarify before manager runs cascade alone.

---

## Slide 14: Closing

**Time:** 0:55–1:00

**Facilitator says:**

> "Three things to walk away with: <reads bullets>. Cascade kit prep starts when it lands Dec 11. Read it. The cascade prompts are the script — don't deviate. The team meeting is discussion-led, not training-led; that means you LISTEN more than you EXPLAIN. Questions?"

**Audience does:** Final Q&A. Facilitator addresses what's possible; defers to HRBP queue otherwise.

**Purpose:** Compress takeaways. Set cascade-prep expectation.

---

## Post-session

- Capture follow-up questions in tracking doc; route to HRBP queue
- Confirm cascade kit delivery date with HR Ops (Dec 11)
- Backlash watch: if discussion was unusually heated about top-of-band freeze or 60/40 shift, flag to HRBP
